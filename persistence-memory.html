The Persistence of Memory

Artist: Salvador Dalí

Year: 1931

Country: Spain

Style: Surrealism

Current Location: Museum of Modern Art, New York

The Persistence of Memory is one of Salvador Dalí's most famous paintings. It is known for its melting clocks spread across a dream-like landscape. The artwork explores ideas about time, memory, and reality and remains one of the most famous surrealist paintings ever created.
